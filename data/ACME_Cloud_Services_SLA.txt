SERVICE LEVEL AGREEMENT
ACME Cloud Services, Inc. | Master Services Schedule A — Service Levels

This Service Level Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of March 1, 2026 (the "Effective Date") by and
between ACME Cloud Services, Inc., a Delaware corporation with offices at 1200 Innovation Parkway, Austin,
Texas (the "Provider"), and Northwind Logistics LLC, a limited liability company organized under the laws of the
State of California (the "Customer"). The Provider and the Customer are each a "Party" and together the "Parties."

RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Provider offers cloud infrastructure, managed support, and hardware provisioning services; and
WHEREAS, the Customer wishes to engage the Provider to deliver such services subject to the service levels and
remedies set forth herein;
NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the Parties agree as follows:

1. DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Downtime" means any period during which the production environment is unavailable to the Customer,
excluding Scheduled Maintenance.
1.2 "Measurement Period" means each calendar month during the Term.
1.3 "Scheduled Maintenance" means maintenance windows notified to the Customer at least five (5) business days
in advance.
1.4 "Support Ticket" means a written request for assistance submitted through the Provider's designated support
portal.

2. SERVICE LEVEL COMMITMENTS

2.1 Availability. The Provider guarantees that the production environment shall maintain a monthly uptime of at
least 99.9% during each Measurement Period. Availability is calculated as the total minutes in the Measurement
Period less Downtime, divided by the total minutes, expressed as a percentage.

2.2 Support Responsiveness. The Provider shall deliver a first response to each Support Ticket within two (2) hours
of submission, measured on a twenty-four (24) hour basis, including weekends and holidays.

2.3 Hardware Delivery. Where the Services include provisioning of physical hardware, the Provider shall complete
delivery within forty-eight (48) hours of the Customer's order confirmation.

2.4 Reporting. The Provider shall furnish the Customer with a monthly performance report within ten (10) business
days following the close of each Measurement Period.

3. SERVICE CREDITS AND PENALTIES

3.1 Availability Shortfall. For each hour of Downtime that causes monthly uptime to fall below the 99.9%
guarantee, the Provider shall credit the Customer five hundred dollars ($500) per hour of such Downtime.

3.2 Late Response. Each Support Ticket that does not receive a first response within the committed two-hour
window shall incur a penalty of two hundred fifty dollars ($250) per incident.

3.3 Delivery Delay. Hardware deliveries completed after the forty-eight (48) hour commitment shall be penalized
one hundred dollars ($100) per hour of delay.

3.4 Service credits shall be applied against the following month's invoice and shall constitute the Customer's
primary remedy for service level failures, subject to Section 6.

4. FEES AND PAYMENT

4.1 The Customer shall pay the fees set forth in the applicable Order Form. All invoices are due net thirty (30) days
from the invoice date.

4.2 Amounts not paid when due shall accrue interest at a rate of 1.5% per month, or the maximum rate permitted by
law, whichever is lower.

5. TERM AND TERMINATION

5.1 This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for an initial term of twelve (12) months,
renewing automatically for successive twelve (12) month terms unless either Party provides written notice of
non-renewal at least thirty (30) days prior to the end of the then-current term.

5.2 Either Party may terminate this Agreement for a material breach that remains uncured thirty (30) days after
written notice of such breach.

6. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

6.1 Except for breaches of confidentiality, each Party's aggregate liability arising out of or related to this Agreement
shall not exceed fifty thousand dollars ($50,000).

6.2 In no event shall either Party be liable for indirect, incidental, or consequential damages, even if advised of the
possibility of such damages.

7. CONFIDENTIALITY

7.1 Each Party shall protect the other's Confidential Information using no less than reasonable care and shall not
disclose it to third parties except as required to perform under this Agreement or as compelled by law.

8. GOVERNING LAW

8.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware,
without regard to its conflict of laws principles.
